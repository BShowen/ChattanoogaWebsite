History

Chattanooga was originally inhabited by Native Americans with sites dating back as far as 10,000 BCE. During the American Civil War Chattanooga was the center for battle with the railway serving over 50% of the Confederacy's military equipment. There wer three Battles of Chattanooga fought in Tennessee during the American Civil War.

The First Battle of Chattanooga

This was a minor artillery battle fought from June 7th to June 8th, 1862.

The Second Battle of Chattanooga

This was the opening battle in the Chickamauga Campaign. The Battles of Chattanooga are the larger and more famous battles which included the Battle of Lookout Mountain and the Battle of Missionary Ridge.

Battle of Lookout Mountain

The Battle of Lookout Mountain started on November 24, 1863. Confederate forces were defeated by Union forces. You can visit lookout mountain and see some of the cannons and military houses used during this battle.

Battle of Missionary Ridge

The Battle of Missionary Ridge was fought the day after The Battle of Lookout Mountain. The Union army, again, defeating the Confederacy.
